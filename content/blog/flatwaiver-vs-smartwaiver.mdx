## Smartwaiver at a glance

Credit where it's due — Smartwaiver earned its reputation:

- **Native iOS/Android kiosk apps with offline mode.** Tablets keep collecting signatures with no internet, which matters for tour operators and venues with flaky Wi-Fi.
- **Deep ecosystem.** Barcode key-tag check-in, event management, and integrations with booking and marketing tools built up over many years.
- **Maturity signals.** A long operating history and a published SOC 2 Type II attestation — meaningful reassurance for larger or compliance-sensitive buyers.

If your operation is built around those capabilities, Smartwaiver is a safe, capable choice.

When a waiver is ever questioned, the real questions are *what exactly did they see, when, and can you prove the record is unaltered?* FlatWaiver answers all three by construction — a once-rendered PDF with a SHA-256 hash, waiver text pinned to the exact version signed, and the consent statement captured. *(Enforceability also depends on your state and your wording — [we cover what actually holds up](/blog/are-digital-waivers-legally-binding), and it's not legal advice; have a lawyer review your waiver text.)*

## Where Smartwaiver is better

An honest comparison has to say this plainly:

- **Offline signing.** FlatWaiver's kiosk is web-based and needs a connection; Smartwaiver's native apps collect offline. If you run tablets in a spotty-Wi-Fi environment or take waivers in the field, that's a real advantage.
- **Integrations & hardware.** Established booking/marketing integrations and barcode key-tag check-in. If your front desk is already wired around those, the switching cost is genuine.
- **Compliance paperwork.** A published SOC 2 Type II attestation and a long track record — worth more to some enterprise and franchise buyers than any pricing difference.
- **Very low volume.** Under ~100 waivers/month, Smartwaiver Basic ($19) is cheaper than flat $39. Below the crossover, per-waiver pricing wins — and we'll say so.
